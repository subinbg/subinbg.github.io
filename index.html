Favorite Subject

I love quantum chemistry and algebra; in fact, they are highly interconnected to each other. For instance, from the Peter-Weyl theorem:

Peter-Weyl Theorem.

Let \(G\) be a compact (Lie) group. Then, every irreducible representation \(\rho\) of \(G\) is necessarily finite-dimensional. Furthermore, \(\rho\) is unitary (or, equivalent to unitary) and every representation of \(G\) is reducible, splitting into \(\rho\)'s. The matrix coefficients of all \(\rho\)'s form an orthogonal (or orthonormal, if normalized) basis of \(\mathcal{L}^2 (G)\).

, we can derive that the one-electron wave function of a Hydrogen atom consists of \(s\), \(p\), \(d\), \(\cdots\) orbitals (actually, the Peter-Weyl theorem is one of my favorite equations!). In addition, I have studied the Hilbert space formalism of quantum mechanics; if you are interested, please see: Mathematics of Modern Physics.
